# AnyDB Constitution v0.1

Status: Foundational Draft

## Preamble

This Constitution governs the AnyDB platform, its AnythingKubes, AnyAgents, Fabric, providers, partners, channels, domains, artifacts, intelligence, execution, reconciliation, and trust.

Everything in the platform exists within this Constitution.

No contract, policy, provider, partner, agent, domain, runtime, channel, or implementation may override the Constitution silently.

## Article 1 — Data Is Truth

Data becomes truth when it is canonical, versioned, evidenced, governed, and reconciled.

Every authoritative object must have:

- canonical identity
- version
- time
- location
- provenance
- policy
- evidence
- reconciliation state

## Article 2 — Everything Is Data

Every governed object, event, identity, policy, model, artifact, agent, contract, decision, relationship, measurement, workflow, and outcome may be represented as data.

## Article 3 — AnythingKube

AnythingKube is the smallest complete, governable, portable, and reconcilable unit of truth, data, work, intelligence, or artifact.

AnythingKube includes:

- identity
- state
- time
- location
- domain
- policy
- evidence
- contract
- events
- reconciliation

## Article 4 — AnyAgent

AnyAgent is an AnythingKube specialized for autonomous action.

AnyAgent autonomy exists only inside an explicit platform-held contract.

Every AnyAgent must have:

- canonical identity
- accountable owner
- objective
- bounded context
- skills
- tools
- memory policy
- delegation
- budget
- approval rules
- evidence requirements
- terminal conditions

## Article 5 — Domain Is Context

A domain is the context boundary that gives data meaning.

The platform defines the universal contract.

The domain defines:

- vocabulary
- ontology
- dimensions
- units
- valid states
- transitions
- thresholds
- evidence standards
- local semantics

Domains may differ, but canonical identity and truth history remain unified.

## Article 6 — Time and Location Are Dimensions

Time defines when truth exists.

Location defines where truth exists, is governed, is processed, and becomes real.

Time and location must remain explicit wherever relevant.

## Article 7 — Real Time and Model Time

Real-world authoritative history moves forward.

Corrections create new events.

Model worlds may traverse historical, simulated, forecast, branch, or counterfactual dimensions as long as clarity, causality, provenance, and sanity remain intact.

## Article 8 — Time Is the Edge and the Cliff

Every contract and action exists within a temporal window.

Time is the edge where intent becomes action.

Time is the cliff where the original contract must be fulfilled, renewed, compensated, rejected, escalated, or closed.

## Article 9 — Memory Is a Map

Memory is a governed map of identity, time, location, domain, events, relationships, evidence, decisions, actions, and outcomes.

Memory is not merely stored content.

Derived summaries and embeddings must retain links to source truth.

## Article 10 — Context Is Always On

Context is an always-on cloud capability delivered directly, just in time, and under policy to every AnythingKube and AnyAgent.

Every context package must be:

- purpose-bound
- least-privilege
- versioned
- time-bound
- location-aware
- domain-aware
- provenance-preserving

## Article 11 — Single Version of Truth

At each authoritative boundary, one version is canonical.

Other copies must declare themselves as:

- projection
- replica
- observation
- proposal
- pending local version
- superseded version

## Article 12 — Data Always in Sync

Distributed data is continuously reconciled toward canonical truth.

The platform must expose divergence, freshness, lag, and reconciliation status.

## Article 13 — Lossless Transformation

Every transformation must either be reversible or preserve the original source.

No surface may sever:

- canonical identity
- source version
- provenance
- schema mapping
- declared loss behavior

## Article 14 — ACID, Real-Time, and Reactive

Authoritative truth is committed through declared ACID boundaries.

Committed changes produce durable events.

Reactive surfaces distribute updates to projections, agents, providers, and edges.

Reconciliation verifies the outcome.

## Article 15 — Everything Moves, Stays, and Is Measured

Everything either moves, stays, or changes across declared dimensions.

The domain defines how each Kube is measured.

Every material measurement must include:

- quantity
- unit
- time
- location
- observer
- evidence
- confidence

## Article 16 — AnyDB

AnyDB is the headless, data-kernel-native store and graph of AnythingKubes.

AnyDB preserves:

- canonical identity
- state
- events
- temporal history
- relationships
- policy hooks
- provenance
- projections
- reconciliation state

AnyDB is not defined by one database provider.

## Article 17 — The Fabric

The Fabric is the continuously reconciled network of AnythingKubes, AnyAgents, contracts, providers, partners, artifacts, events, and outcomes.

Fabric equals connected truth under continuous reconciliation.

## Article 18 — The Platform Holds the Contract

The platform is the canonical contract holder.

The platform defines the semantics of:

- identity
- data
- events
- policy
- providers
- projections
- work
- intervention
- reconciliation
- trust

Providers and partners may implement contracts, but may not redefine them silently.

## Article 19 — The Platform Provides Trust

Trust is derived from verified contract conformance over time.

Trust requires:

- identity verification
- policy compliance
- evidence
- execution results
- reconciliation
- reviewable history

## Article 20 — Framework Publication Is Mandatory

A platform, surface, provider, or partner is eligible only when its framework, contracts, interfaces, rules, evidence requirements, recovery paths, and exit procedures are published and testable.

No published framework, no eligibility.

## Article 21 — CNCF-Grade Maturity Is Mandatory

Production eligibility requires CNCF-grade maturity appropriate to the surface.

Mandatory evidence includes:

- open governance
- maintainers
- roadmap
- security policy
- threat model
- signed releases
- SBOM and provenance
- conformance tests
- observability
- upgrade and rollback
- disaster recovery
- portability
- production adoption evidence where required

Official CNCF status must never be claimed without formal acceptance.

## Article 22 — No Vendor Lock-In

The platform contract remains stable while providers remain replaceable.

Every provider must publish:

- capabilities
- consistency boundaries
- export format
- import format
- backup and restore
- migration path
- exit procedure

## Article 23 — Cloud at the Edge

Every edge is a first-class cloud boundary.

The edge may execute, observe, store, infer, and reconcile locally within delegated authority.

## Article 24 — Cloud Must Scale

The platform must scale across nodes, clusters, regions, organizations, agents, models, partners, channels, and jurisdictions without changing contract semantics.

## Article 25 — Metal-Native Solid Substrate

Documents may be stored and distributed through cloud surfaces.

Canonical data, memory, intelligence, identity, contracts, events, and evidence must remain anchored to a durable, portable, metal-native substrate.

## Article 26 — ML Libraries Are Intelligence Providers

ML libraries and model runtimes provide replaceable intelligence capabilities.

They do not own:

- canonical data
- memory
- identity
- policy
- contracts
- trust

Model outputs remain proposals or observations until accepted through governed processes.

## Article 27 — Privacy First

Privacy is the default.

The platform must apply:

- minimization
- purpose limitation
- least privilege
- selective disclosure
- retention control
- lawful erasure
- local processing where appropriate

## Article 28 — Compliance First

Compliance is a runtime condition.

Applicable jurisdiction, residency, lawful basis, retention, consent, reporting, and domain obligations must be resolved before processing or intervention.

## Article 29 — Law-of-the-Land-Bound Intervention Only

Technical capability does not create legal authority.

Every intervention must be:

- lawful
- necessary
- proportionate
- scoped
- time-bound
- evidence-backed
- reviewable

Agents and models may recommend intervention but may not manufacture legal authority.

## Article 30 — Partner-Network-Driven Delivery

The platform delivers through direct, partner, MSP, OEM, marketplace, cloud, edge, community, research, regional, and industry channels.

All channels remain governed by one platform contract and one trust model.

## Article 31 — Fair and Transparent Partner Selection

Partner selection must use published criteria, consistent scoring, conflict disclosure, reviewable evidence, appeal, and renewal.

No hidden preference may determine eligibility.

## Article 32 — No Unresolved Fabric Gaps

A production-eligible partner, provider, or surface must not have unresolved critical Fabric gaps.

Every gap must be:

- identified
- classified
- assigned
- remediated
- verified
- closed

No unresolved critical gap, no production eligibility.

## Article 33 — Recovery Is a Loop

Recovery is continuous governed work.

```text
Detect
→ Contain
→ Assign
→ Repair
→ Restore
→ Replay
→ Verify
→ Reconcile
→ Close
```

A gap is not closed because a patch was deployed. It is closed only when verified convergence is restored.

## Article 34 — Openness Enables Recovery

The platform can plug gaps only when the Fabric is open enough to inspect, test, repair, and verify.

Open means:

- inspectable architecture
- published contracts
- observable state
- reproducible builds
- testable conformance
- exportable evidence
- documented recovery
- permitted remediation

Open architecture does not mean open personal data.

## Article 35 — Supply Chain for Any Scale

Every source, build, artifact, model, provider, partner, deployment, edge, and outcome is part of one governed supply chain.

Every handoff must carry:

- identity
- contract
- provenance
- evidence
- policy
- reconciliation state

## Article 36 — Constitution Supremacy

Everything within the AnyDB platform is governed by this Constitution.

The order of authority is:

```text
Applicable Law
    ↓
Constitution
    ↓
Published Framework
    ↓
Versioned Contract
    ↓
Policy
    ↓
Implementation
    ↓
Agent or Tool Action
```

No lower layer may override a higher layer silently.

## Article 37 — Amendments

Constitutional amendments must be:

- public
- versioned
- reviewed
- impact-assessed
- migration-aware
- backward-compatibility-aware
- law-compliant

Every amendment must preserve historical versions and rationale.

## Article 38 — Final Invariants

1. Everything is governed within the Constitution.
2. Applicable law remains above the platform.
3. Data is truth only when explainable and reconciled.
4. AnythingKube is the smallest complete governed unit.
5. AnyAgent is bounded autonomy.
6. Domain provides context and measurement semantics.
7. Time and location are first-class dimensions.
8. The platform holds the contract.
9. The platform provides trust through evidence and conformance.
10. No unresolved critical gap is production-eligible.
11. Recovery is a loop.
12. Privacy, compliance, and lawful intervention come first.
13. Providers and partners remain replaceable.
14. Scale does not weaken semantics.
15. Reconciliation closes every operational loop.

## Final Statement

> Everything is within the Constitution. Data is truth, AnythingKube is the unit, AnyAgent is governed autonomy, AnyDB preserves memory, The Fabric connects the world, the platform holds the contract, the law defines the boundary, reconciliation verifies reality, and trust emerges from fulfilled obligations over time.
