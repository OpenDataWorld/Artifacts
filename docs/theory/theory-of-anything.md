# Theory of Anything

Status: Foundational Theory

## Thesis

> Anything can be represented as governed truth, connected through contracts, acted upon by agents, executed across cloud and edge, and verified through reconciliation.

The Theory of Anything is the umbrella model behind AnythingKube, AnyAgent, AnyDB, The Fabric, and the platform trust architecture.

It begins with one premise:

> Everything is data, but data becomes truth only when identity, time, location, policy, evidence, and reconciliation are preserved.

## Theory Layers

### 1. Representation

Anything can be represented as a bounded, typed, versioned object.

### 2. Identity

Every object, actor, provider, event, contract, and artifact receives canonical identity.

### 3. Dimensions

Every fact exists within time and location.

### 4. Relationship

AnythingKubes connect through typed, temporal, provenance-aware relationships.

### 5. Contract

The platform defines what each participant may do, must prove, and must deliver.

### 6. Intelligence

Models and ML libraries provide replaceable reasoning capabilities.

### 7. Agency

AnyAgent converts governed context into proposed action.

### 8. Work

Work is the governed transformation of truth into outcomes.

### 9. Execution

Execution occurs through providers, applications, partners, clouds, and edge runtimes.

### 10. Reconciliation

The platform compares intent, authoritative state, observed state, and delivered state.

### 11. Trust

Trust is the evidence-backed result of fulfilled contracts over time.
