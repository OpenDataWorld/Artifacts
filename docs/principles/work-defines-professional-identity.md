# AnyDB Foundation Principle: Work Defines Professional Identity

Status: Normative Platform Principle

## Principle

> In the platform economy, participants are known through their work. Work is the strongest evidence of capability, contribution, reliability, maturity, and value.

A person’s, agent’s, operator’s, organization’s, or domain’s professional identity is formed by what it has actually built, delivered, maintained, improved, recovered, documented, taught, and contributed.

Work may determine professional reputation, network position, destination, connections, eligibility, and earned access.

However, work does not determine basic human dignity, legal identity, fundamental rights, or moral worth.

## Core Rule

> Claims introduce you. Work proves you. Outcomes establish trust.

## Canonical Model

```text
Identity
   ↓
Declared Capability
   ↓
Work
   ↓
Evidence
   ↓
Outcome
   ↓
Reputation
   ↓
Trust
   ↓
Connections and Earned Access
```

## Work as Evidence

Work may include:

- original artifacts
- research
- software
- designs
- decisions
- operations
- maintenance
- recovery
- teaching
- documentation
- community contributions
- partner delivery
- customer outcomes
- governance work

Every work item should preserve:

- creator or operator identity
- domain
- purpose
- contract
- time
- contribution scope
- collaborators
- evidence
- outcome
- ownership
- license
- provenance

## Work-Defined Identity Card

```text
Professional Identity Card
=
Skills
+ Portfolio
+ Verified Work
+ Outcomes
+ Contribution History
+ Trust
```

Registries should contain only aliases, profiles, references, and verification status. The portfolio and evidence remain with their lawful owner or custodian.

## Work and Capability

A claimed capability becomes credible through demonstrated work.

Recommended states:

```text
claimed
demonstrated
verified
operationalized
industrialized
federated-and-generational
```

## Work and Reputation

Reputation must be contextual.

A strong record in one domain does not automatically establish authority in another.

```text
Reputation
=
Work
+ Domain
+ Time
+ Outcome
+ Evidence
```

## Work and Network

Work shapes the professional network by connecting participants through:

- shared projects
- dependencies
- contributions
- reviews
- contracts
- referrals
- research
- delivery
- recovery
- knowledge transfer

Connections should be attributable and purpose-bound, not manufactured through opaque popularity alone.

## Work and Destination

A participant’s next destination may be influenced by:

- demonstrated capability
- maturity
- trust
- portfolio relevance
- domain fit
- verified outcomes
- learning progression
- current profile

The platform may recommend opportunities, operators, communities, domains, or challenges based on this evidence.

## Work and Access

Access may be earned through verified work, but must remain:

- purpose-bound
- proportional
- revocable
- time-bound
- domain-specific
- policy-governed
- reviewable

Work history does not grant unrestricted authority.

## Fairness

A work-defined identity system must account for unequal opportunity.

It should not punish participants for lacking access to privileged institutions, networks, tools, capital, or formal credentials.

The platform should recognize:

- self-directed work
- community work
- caregiving and maintenance work where relevant
- open-source contribution
- informal learning
- recovery and repair
- local and cultural knowledge
- verified capability without conventional credentials

## No Popularity Substitution

```text
Popularity
    ≠
Capability

Visibility
    ≠
Value

Credential
    ≠
Outcome
```

Professional trust should rely on attributable work and evidence.

## Collaborative Work

Collaborative work must preserve individual and collective attribution.

The platform should distinguish:

- author
- owner
- maintainer
- contributor
- reviewer
- operator
- sponsor
- accountable contract holder

No participant may appropriate the work of others.

## AnyAgent Work Identity

An AnyAgent is known by:

- assigned objective
- completed work
- quality
- safety
- evidence
- recovery performance
- tool discipline
- contract conformance
- lifecycle history

Its owner remains accountable for delegated authority and real-world consequences.

## Natural Death and Graceful Exit

When an agent or operator exits:

- authority ends
- work history remains
- provenance remains
- portfolio remains governed
- reusable knowledge may continue under contract
- successor identity remains distinct

## Privacy

Not all work must be public.

The platform should support:

- public portfolio references
- private evidence
- confidential customer work
- selective disclosure
- verified claims without exposing protected content
- anonymized or pseudonymous work where lawful

## Human Dignity Boundary

The platform must never infer that a person without visible work lacks identity, dignity, rights, or worth.

```text
Professional Identity
    may be evidenced by work

Human Dignity
    is not conditional on work
```

## Platform Invariants

1. Professional identity is grounded in attributable work.
2. Capability claims require evidence.
3. Reputation is domain- and time-specific.
4. Work shapes professional connections and earned access.
5. Access remains bounded, revocable, and governed.
6. Collaborative contributions preserve attribution.
7. Popularity and credentials do not replace outcomes.
8. Unequal opportunity is considered in evaluation.
9. Agents are evaluated through work while owners remain accountable.
10. Human dignity and fundamental rights never depend on productivity or portfolio.

## Final Statement

> You are professionally known by your work. Your work shapes your capability profile, reputation, network, connections, destination, and earned access. Claims may open the gate, but only attributable work and evidenced outcomes establish trust. Human dignity, identity, and rights remain unconditional.
