# Common Cause Clusters

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> A Common Cause Cluster is a governed, temporary or persistent federation of independently accountable members who organize around one clearly defined cause, destination, public purpose, problem, risk, or opportunity.

The cause is shared. Identity, sovereignty, responsibility, authority, and provenance remain distinct.

## Core Rule

> Unite around the cause. Preserve the members. Admit through trusted gates. Deliver measurable value. Dissolve or renew when the cause changes or ends.

## Canonical Model

```text
Shared Cause
    ↓
Published Cause Definition
    ↓
Registry Identity
    ↓
Eligible and Able Members
    ↓
Trusted Gate Admission
    ↓
Common Cause Cluster
    ↓
Coordinated Work Kubes
    ↓
Measured Outcome
    ↓
Renew / Reconfigure / Dissolve
```

## Cause Definition

Every cluster begins with a cause that declares:

- cause identity;
- accountable publisher;
- purpose;
- current coordinates;
- intended destination;
- domain and jurisdictions;
- affected people and systems;
- success criteria;
- known constraints;
- evidence requirements;
- consequence class;
- time window;
- expiry;
- exit conditions.

The cause must be published and registered before gate admission.

## What May Form Around a Common Cause

Members may include:

- humans;
- communities;
- organizations;
- nations;
- statutory agencies;
- platforms;
- domain-bound agents;
- research groups;
- public institutions;
- providers;
- tools;
- devices;
- knowledge and data stewards.

Every member resolves to a Kube and enters independently through the gate.

## Membership Rule

Membership requires:

1. verified identity;
2. alignment with the declared cause;
3. accepted cluster contract;
4. valid jurisdiction and authority;
5. eligibility;
6. ability;
7. maturity appropriate to the role;
8. maker-checker and conflict review where required;
9. explicit responsibility;
10. provenance and evidence obligations;
11. destination acceptance;
12. expiry and exit path.

Support for the same cause does not create unrestricted trust between members.

## Cluster Contract

The Common Cause Cluster contract must define:

- cause and destination;
- member identities;
- accountable organization, council, or federation;
- roles and duties;
- decision and quorum rules;
- separation of duties;
- collective outcome responsibility;
- operating modes;
- resource and budget limits;
- data-sharing boundaries;
- intellectual-property and commercial rights;
- open publishing rules;
- metering and contributor support;
- handoff rules;
- validation gates;
- one eligible committer for each real transition;
- dispute, recovery, suspension, expiry, and dissolution.

## Cause Before Institution

The cluster exists to serve the cause, not to preserve itself indefinitely.

```text
Cause
    >
Cluster Permanence
```

When the cluster becomes disconnected from the cause, it must be reviewed, redefined, restricted, or dissolved.

## Common Cause Does Not Erase Difference

Members may retain:

- local governance;
- distinct beliefs and methods;
- domain language;
- national or organizational sovereignty;
- data custody;
- proprietary intelligence;
- commercial rights;
- independent exit.

Interoperability requires only the minimum common contract necessary to advance the cause.

## Decision Model

Many members may reason, propose, test, and validate.

```text
Collective Deliberation
    ↓
Gate Consolidation
    ↓
Validated Decision
    ↓
One Eligible Execution Kube
    ↓
One Durable Outcome
```

No cause justifies unbounded authority, self-approval, or the removal of rights.

## Responsibility

Duties remain separated, while the outcome is collectively owned.

```text
One owner per action.
Many responsible contributors per outcome.
```

The cluster must identify:

- who defined;
- who approved;
- who executed;
- who validated;
- who audited;
- who owns recovery;
- who owns the final outcome.

## Trusted Gates

Every material crossing validates:

```text
Identity
+ Context
+ Contract
+ Cause Alignment
+ Jurisdiction
+ Eligibility
+ Ability
+ Responsibility
+ Provenance
+ Exit
```

Members retain the Right to Reason and lawful Right to Deny.

## Common Cause and Nations

Nations may form Common Cause Clusters for:

- disaster response;
- public health;
- research;
- education;
- climate and environmental resilience;
- infrastructure;
- trade;
- standards;
- digital safety;
- cultural and knowledge exchange.

Each nation participates through its own lawful gate and retains constitutional governance, jurisdiction, sovereignty, and exit.

## Economic Model

Usage may be metered and value distributed through a declared settlement contract.

The economic model should support:

- contributors;
- infrastructure;
- assurance;
- governance;
- maintenance;
- recovery;
- public-interest access;
- long-term preservation;
- responsible commercial delivery.

Open participation does not remove attribution, ownership, or commercial rights.

## Continuous Reconciliation

The platform must compare:

```text
Declared Cause and Commitments
        ↕
Observed Work and Outcomes
        ↓
Aligned / Drifted / Blocked / Completed / Expired
```

Cause drift triggers review and revalidation.

## Cluster Lifecycle

```text
proposed
→ published
→ registered
→ forming
→ active
→ reviewed
→ renewed
→ reconfigured
→ completed
→ dissolved
→ archived
```

No cluster remains active merely because it once served a valid cause.

## Completion and Dissolution

The cluster must close or re-charter when:

- success criteria are met;
- the cause is no longer valid;
- the destination changes materially;
- the contract expires;
- responsibility coverage fails;
- conformance or trust degrades;
- no valid path remains;
- the cluster creates more friction or harm than value.

Dissolution preserves:

- outcomes;
- instructions;
- decisions;
- contribution records;
- rights and settlements;
- evidence;
- unresolved obligations;
- provenance;
- lessons for future clusters.

## Platform Invariants

1. Every cluster is organized around one explicitly defined common cause.
2. The cause is published and registered before admission.
3. Every member remains independently identifiable and accountable.
4. Membership requires Eligibility and Ability.
5. Common purpose never creates unrestricted trust or authority.
6. Duties remain separated and outcomes are collectively owned.
7. Every consequential crossing uses a Trusted Gate.
8. Every real transition has one eligible execution Kube.
9. Rights, sovereignty, proprietary intelligence, and lawful exit remain preserved.
10. Contributions, usage, value, and outcomes remain attributable.
11. Cause drift triggers revalidation.
12. The cluster renews, reconfigures, or dissolves when the cause or conditions change.

## Final Statement

> Common Cause Clusters allow people, agents, organizations, platforms, domains, and nations to organize around a real shared purpose without erasing their identities or sovereignty. The cause provides direction, the gate provides governance, the Kube provides accountable work, and measured delivery earns trust. When the cause is complete or no longer coherent, the cluster must close, reconfigure, or begin again under a new contract.
