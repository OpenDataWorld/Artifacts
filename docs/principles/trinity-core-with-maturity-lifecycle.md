# AnyDB Foundation Principle: Trinity at the Core, Maturity for Every Additional Dimension

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> The platform has a trinity at its core. Every additional dimension must pass through a defined maturity lifecycle before it can become part of that core.

The core remains small, stable, interoperable, and trusted. New capabilities, models, profiles, surfaces, operators, protocols, and domains may begin at the edge, but they do not become foundational merely because they exist or appear useful.

## The Core Trinity

The core trinity is:

```text
Identity
Context
Contract
```

### Identity

Identity answers:

- who or what exists
- who owns or controls it
- who is accountable
- which lifecycle state applies

### Context

Context answers:

- what the thing means here
- which domain applies
- what time, place, purpose, assumptions, and constraints are active

### Contract

Contract answers:

- what may happen
- who may act
- under which conditions
- what evidence, limits, quality, recovery, and terminal state are required

## Core Formula

```text
Core
=
Identity
+ Context
+ Contract
```

This trinity makes a Kube governable.

```text
Kube

```

## Maturity Lifecycle

Every new dimension must progress through:

```text
proposed
→ defined
→ modeled
→ simulated
→ demonstrated
→ verified
→ conformant
→ operationalized
→ industrialized
→ federated
→ core-eligible
```

### Proposed

The idea and intended value are declared.

### Defined

Meaning, owner, scope, assumptions, and boundaries are explicit.

### Modeled

The dimension is represented in a coherent world model.

### Simulated

Multiple virtual trials test behavior, failure, and interactions.

### Demonstrated

The capability works in a bounded environment.

### Verified

Identity, evidence, security, quality, and known-fact consistency are checked.

### Conformant

The dimension satisfies applicable kernel, domain, legal, protocol, and enterprise requirements.

### Operationalized

It works repeatedly in production with observability, support, recovery, and accountable ownership.

### Industrialized

It scales across workloads, operators, channels, regions, and providers with enterprise-grade quality assurance.

### Federated

It interoperates across independent domains and organizations without losing identity, context, contract, provenance, or local sovereignty.

### Core-Eligible

It has demonstrated enough stability, universality, backward compatibility, and long-term value to be considered for the core.

## Core Admission Test

A dimension may become core only when it is:

- universally or broadly necessary
- clearly defined
- stable across versions
- backward-compatible
- provider-neutral
- interoperable
- observable
- recoverable
- secure
- privacy-preserving
- enterprise-grade
- federated
- evidenced by repeated real-world value
- governed by a published lifecycle and retirement path

## No Direct Promotion

```text
New Idea
    ≠
Core Principle
```

No dimension may move directly from proposal to core.

Core admission requires evidence across the full maturity lifecycle.

## Stable Core, Fluid Edge

```text
Core
  = small, stable, trusted

Edge
  = experimental, diverse, fast-moving
```

The edge explores many possibilities.

The core accepts only proven invariants.

## Demotion and Retirement

Core status is not permanent.

A core dimension may be:

```text
active
→ under-review
→ deprecated
→ compatibility-only
→ retired
→ archived
```

Demotion may be triggered by:

- loss of relevance
- security failure
- better universal abstraction
- incompatibility
- excessive complexity
- broken federation
- insufficient value

Retirement must preserve historical interpretation and migration paths.

## Human at the Wall

Core admission and removal require accountable human governance.

The human at the wall must be able to:

- inspect evidence
- challenge assumptions
- request more trials
- approve or reject admission
- define conditions
- stop promotion
- require remediation
- trigger retirement

## Platform Invariants

1. The core trinity is Identity, Context, and Contract.
2. The core remains intentionally small and stable.
3. Every additional dimension begins outside the core.
4. Every dimension follows a maturity lifecycle.
5. No capability becomes core through popularity or novelty alone.
6. Core admission requires repeated real-world value and enterprise-grade evidence.
7. Backward compatibility and interoperability are mandatory.
8. Human governance controls core admission and retirement.
9. Core dimensions may be deprecated and retired gracefully.
10. The edge remains open for experimentation while the core protects continuity.

## Final Statement

> Identity, Context, and Contract form the trinity at the core. Every additional dimension must earn its place through a full maturity lifecycle—from proposal and simulation to verification, production, federation, and sustained value. The edge may evolve rapidly; the core changes only when evidence proves that the new dimension has become a stable foundation.
