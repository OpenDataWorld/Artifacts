# Everything Is Dated; Ideas Move Freely

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> Everything is dated, and ideas should move freely.

Every idea, claim, model, contract, artifact, decision, implementation, and correction must carry time, authorship, provenance, version, and context. At the same time, ideas should be free to travel across people, communities, enterprises, industries, nations, platforms, and generations for discussion, verification, adaptation, and innovation.

## Core Rule

> Date the artifact. Credit the origin. Preserve the lineage. Let the idea move.

## Canonical Model

```text
Idea
    ↓
Authored and Dated Artifact
    ↓
Published Metadata and Rights
    ↓
Open Discovery and Debate
    ↓
Native Adaptation
    ↓
Verification and Implementation
    ↓
New Dated Version with Provenance
```

## Everything Is Dated

Every artifact should declare:

- creation time;
- publication time;
- effective time;
- last review time;
- expiry or next review;
- author or finder;
- owner;
- publisher;
- version;
- source context;
- applicable contract;
- supersession history;
- current lifecycle state.

```text
Meaning
=
Content
+ Context
+ Time
+ Version
```

An idea without time may be mistaken for a timeless truth. A policy without an effective date may be applied outside its authority. A model without versioning cannot be verified or reproduced.

## Ideas as Artifacts

An idea becomes an artifact when it has:

- canonical identity;
- title and description;
- author or origin;
- date;
- version;
- provenance;
- domain;
- assumptions;
- evidence;
- rights and license;
- review state;
- relationships to prior and later ideas.

```text
Idea Artifact
=
Idea
+ Identity
+ Date
+ Credit
+ Provenance
+ Rights
```

## Free Movement of Ideas

Ideas should be able to move through:

- publication;
- discussion;
- translation;
- education;
- research;
- critique;
- independent verification;
- adaptation;
- implementation;
- federation;
- commercial licensing;
- derivative work.

Movement must preserve attribution, provenance, rights, and the distinction between the original idea and later interpretations.

## Ideas Are Not the Same as Protected Assets

```text
Idea
    = may be discussed and understood

Expression
    = may carry copyright

Implementation
    = may carry commercial and technical rights

Data
    = may carry ownership, privacy, and access restrictions

Brand
    = may carry trademark rights
```

Free movement of ideas does not require unrestricted copying of protected code, content, datasets, identities, secrets, or commercial assets.

## Native Versions

An idea may become native to a new entity, domain, language, culture, jurisdiction, or platform.

Each native version must declare:

- the source idea;
- the transformation;
- the new context;
- the adapter or author;
- changes and omissions;
- retained meaning;
- new evidence;
- rights;
- date and version.

```text
Native Idea Version
=
Source Idea
+ New Context
+ Declared Transformation
+ Preserved Provenance
```

## Truth Preservation

Ideas may evolve, but no version may silently alter the source truth.

The platform must distinguish:

```text
original claim
interpretation
translation
adaptation
critique
correction
implementation
superseding idea
```

Every correction creates a new dated record rather than rewriting history.

## Credit

The platform must preserve credit for:

- originator;
- finder of truth;
- evidence provider;
- validator;
- critic;
- corrector;
- translator;
- implementer;
- maintainer;
- publisher.

Ideas may move freely without making their contributors invisible.

## Open Debate

Free-moving ideas must remain open to:

- challenge;
- alternative hypotheses;
- requests for evidence;
- independent replication;
- disagreement;
- correction;
- supersession.

```text
Free Movement
+ Open Debate
+ Durable Credit
=
Healthy Knowledge Expansion
```

## Commercial Use

Ideas may be commercialized through implementations, services, certifications, tools, managed platforms, education, support, and distribution.

Commercial use must preserve:

- source attribution;
- declared rights;
- contributor agreements;
- reserved commercial rights;
- licensing boundaries;
- settlement where contracted.

```text
Open Idea
    can coexist with
Protected Implementation
```

## Time-Bounded Validity

Every operationalized idea should declare whether it is:

```text
draft
experimental
validated
accepted
operational
legacy
superseded
expired
archived
```

A once-valid idea may become stale when evidence, context, technology, law, or reality changes.

## Universal Indexed Access

Every publishable idea artifact should be indexable through open metadata where lawful.

Indexed means discoverable. It does not automatically mean unrestricted access to protected implementation details, private evidence, personal data, or confidential content.

## Platform Responsibilities

The platform must:

- assign canonical identities;
- preserve dates and versions;
- maintain provenance;
- credit contributors;
- expose relationships among ideas;
- support translation and native adaptation;
- preserve rights and licenses;
- enable debate and correction;
- distinguish ideas from protected assets;
- support export, federation, and exit;
- prevent silent historical rewriting.

## Platform Invariants

1. Every artifact is dated and versioned.
2. Every idea has resolvable origin and provenance.
3. Ideas may move across domains and platforms.
4. Movement preserves credit, rights, and source lineage.
5. Free ideas do not imply unrestricted copying of protected assets.
6. Native adaptations remain linked to their source.
7. Corrections and supersessions create new forward records.
8. Operational ideas are time-bounded and reviewable.
9. Open debate is protected as a mechanism of improvement.
10. No platform may capture ownership of an idea merely by hosting or distributing it.

## Foundation Formula

```text
Living Idea
=
Dated Artifact

```

```text
Healthy Knowledge Commons
=
Free Ideas

```

## Final Statement

> Everything is dated because meaning, validity, and responsibility exist in time. Ideas should move freely because knowledge grows through discovery, debate, translation, adaptation, verification, and reuse. Date every artifact, credit every contributor, preserve every lineage, protect legitimate rights, and let ideas travel wherever they can help humanity build and verify a better path.
