# Credit the Finder of Truth

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> The person, community, agent, researcher, investigator, validator, or institution that first discovers, verifies, corrects, or preserves a truth must receive durable credit.

Truth belongs to reality. Credit belongs to the finder and those who establish it responsibly.

## Core Rule

> Deliver the truth. Preserve the evidence. Credit the finder.

## Canonical Model

```text
Observation
    ↓
Discovery
    ↓
Evidence
    ↓
Independent Validation
    ↓
Accepted Truth
    ↓
Durable Attribution
    ↓
Reuse, Recognition, and Reward
```

## Finder of Truth

A finder of truth may be:

- the first observer;
- the original researcher;
- the source community;
- a whistleblower acting lawfully;
- a journalist;
- an investigator;
- a domain expert;
- a data steward;
- an agent that identifies a pattern;
- a validator who proves a claim;
- a corrector who exposes an error;
- a maintainer who preserves evidence.

Discovery, verification, and preservation are distinct contributions and should be credited separately.

## Truth Credit Record

Every accepted truth should preserve:

- truth or claim identity;
- original finder;
- discovery time;
- source context;
- supporting evidence;
- validators;
- correction history;
- confidence and status;
- publisher;
- current owner of the record;
- licenses and usage rights;
- recognition or settlement terms.

```text
Truth Credit
=
Finder
+ Evidence
+ Validation
+ Provenance
+ Durable Attribution
```

## Discovery Is Not Ownership of Reality

```text
Finding a Truth
    ≠ Owning the Underlying Reality
```

The finder receives attribution and applicable rights over their work, evidence, publication, or contribution. The truth itself remains available for responsible verification and use according to law, ethics, privacy, and contract.

## Multiple Contributors

A truth may have several contributors:

```text
Observer
Researcher
Evidence Provider
Validator
Reviewer
Corrector
Publisher
Preserver
```

The platform must not collapse these roles into one generic credit.

## First Discovery and Independent Discovery

The record should distinguish:

- first documented discovery;
- earlier but unpublished knowledge;
- independent rediscovery;
- verification;
- correction;
- refinement;
- translation;
- preservation.

Where precedence is disputed, the platform must preserve competing claims and evidence rather than silently assigning credit.

## Agents and AI Systems

An AI system may assist in finding a truth, but credit must remain complete.

The record should identify:

- the human or organization that defined the inquiry;
- the data and source contributors;
- the model or agent used;
- the operator;
- the validator;
- the accountable publisher.

```text
Agent Contribution
    = attributable assistance
not
automatic erasure of human and source credit
```

## Corrections

A person who proves that an accepted claim is false, incomplete, outdated, or misleading deserves explicit correction credit.

```text
Correction Credit
=
Identified Error
+ Evidence
+ Validated Replacement
+ Preserved History
```

Corrections create new forward records. They do not erase the original history or the identity of the corrector.

## Credit at Every Surface

Attribution should travel with the truth across:

- datasets;
- metadata;
- articles;
- research papers;
- dashboards;
- APIs;
- agent responses;
- reports;
- translations;
- summaries;
- commercial products;
- derivative works.

A shorter presentation may simplify the credit display, but the complete provenance must remain resolvable.

## Commercial Use

Commercial use of a discovered truth, dataset, evidence package, or validated contribution must respect:

- attribution;
- license;
- reserved rights;
- contributor agreements;
- compensation or revenue share where contracted;
- community and indigenous knowledge protections;
- privacy and confidentiality.

```text
Commercial Value
must not erase
Discovery Credit
```

## Recognition and Reward

Credit may be expressed through:

- named attribution;
- contributor records;
- reputation;
- citations;
- authorship;
- research credit;
- Kube-based contribution records;
- payment;
- royalties;
- grants;
- awards;
- governance participation.

The reward model must be explicit and must not manufacture speculative value detached from the contribution.

## Protection of the Finder

Where discovery creates risk, the platform should support lawful protections such as:

- controlled disclosure;
- pseudonymous public attribution with accountable confidential verification;
- source protection;
- anti-retaliation processes;
- embargoes;
- safety review;
- legal escalation;
- delayed publication.

Protection may limit public identity disclosure, but the trusted accountability chain must remain preserved.

## No Credit Laundering

The platform must reject:

- removing source names;
- replacing the finder with the distributor;
- claiming discovery through mere aggregation;
- presenting verification as original discovery;
- silent appropriation by a platform or model provider;
- citation stripping;
- laundering community knowledge into proprietary ownership.

## Platform Responsibilities

The platform must:

- register truth claims and evidence;
- preserve finder identity and contribution roles;
- maintain immutable provenance;
- support validation and correction;
- carry attribution across transformations and surfaces;
- expose credit disputes;
- support licensing and settlement;
- prevent removal or reassignment of credit without evidence and due process;
- protect finders where lawful and necessary.

## Platform Invariants

1. Every accepted truth has a resolvable provenance chain.
2. The finder of truth receives durable credit.
3. Discovery, evidence, validation, correction, publishing, and preservation are credited separately.
4. Platforms, providers, and distributors cannot appropriate discovery credit.
5. Native versions, summaries, and translations preserve source attribution.
6. Corrections preserve both history and corrector credit.
7. Commercial distribution does not erase attribution or contracted contributor rights.
8. Credit disputes remain visible and evidence-based.
9. Confidentiality may protect the finder without creating faceless accountability.
10. Truth credit is permanent unless corrected through documented evidence and due process.

Fair Knowledge Economy

## Final Statement

> Give credit to the finder of truth. Preserve who observed it, who proved it, who corrected it, who published it, and who kept it available. Truth must not lose its provenance as it moves through platforms, agents, products, and markets. The Fabric delivers the truth and keeps the names of those who brought it into the light.
