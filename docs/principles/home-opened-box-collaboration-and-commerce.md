# Home-Opened Box for Collaboration and Commerce

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> The box should open only in the end user’s trusted home environment. There, the user performs their own work with real-world tools, under local identity, context, law, ownership, and accountability. The result is then packed back into the box, labeled, sealed, and returned to the platform for collaboration, distribution, and commerce.

The platform provides the trusted exchange, registry, contracts, discovery, settlement, and delivery path. It does not need to own the user’s home, tools, intelligence, or creative process.

## Core Rule

> Keep the box sealed in transit. Open it only at the owner’s trusted home. Let the user perform the work natively. Repack the result with evidence and labels. Use the platform for collaboration and commerce.

## The User's Home

The user’s home is the trusted environment in which they retain direct control over:

- identity;
- local context;
- tools;
- data;
- private knowledge;
- workflows;
- creative methods;
- local agents;
- devices and hardware;
- ownership;
- consent;
- privacy;
- recovery and exit.

A home may be:

- a personal device;
- a local workstation;
- a studio;
- a laboratory;
- a workshop;
- an enterprise environment;
- a local edge cluster;
- a community node;
- a sovereign or national domain.

The home environment must be registered when it performs consequential platform work.

## The Sealed Box

Before transit, the box contains only the minimum necessary:

- task or request identity;
- purpose;
- owner;
- requester;
- recipient;
- contract;
- rights;
- constraints;
- expected outcome;
- permitted inputs;
- prohibited actions;
- quality criteria;
- commercial terms;
- expiry;
- provenance requirements;
- recovery and return path.

It must not contain hidden authority, unnecessary surveillance, unrequested software, or unrestricted access to the user’s home.

## Opening at Home

The box may open only after the home gate verifies:

1. box identity and integrity;
2. requester and recipient identities;
3. exact contract version;
4. current ownership;
5. local eligibility and ability;
6. rights and consent;
7. home-environment registration;
8. tool compatibility;
9. commercial and contribution terms;
10. expiry, recovery, and exit.

```text
Transit Box
    = sealed contract and context

Opened Home Box
    = locally accepted work environment
```

## Real-World Tools

The user may perform the work using the tools most native to their craft and environment, including:

- physical tools;
- local software;
- creative applications;
- laboratory equipment;
- business systems;
- local data;
- private models;
- human expertise;
- locally approved agents;
- partner services.

The platform defines the required outcome and evidence, not every internal method, unless safety, compliance, or service terms require a particular procedure.

```text
Contract the Outcome
not
Unnecessarily Control the Craft
```

## The User's Own Magic

The platform should preserve room for:

- judgment;
- creativity;
- craft;
- cultural context;
- domain expertise;
- experimentation;
- personal workflow;
- local innovation.

The user remains responsible for the accepted work while retaining ownership of their protected methods and proprietary intelligence.

## Repacking the Box

After the work is completed, the user repacks the box with:

- resulting artifact or service reference;
- outcome identity;
- owner;
- contributor identities;
- accepted contract version;
- provenance;
- quality and conformance evidence;
- tool and environment metadata required by the contract;
- rights and license;
- commercial terms;
- usage limits;
- delivery instructions;
- expiry;
- support and recovery information.

Private methods, unrelated local data, secrets, and protected intelligence remain outside the box unless explicitly required and lawfully permitted.

- canonical identity;

- domain;
- version;

- rights;
- license;
- commercial use;
- safety classification;
- quality state;
- provenance;
- expiry;
- handling instructions;
- target group;
- delivery methods;
- dispute and support path.

```text
Label
=
Minimum Shared Metadata
for Safe Discovery, Handling, and Commerce
```

## Sealing and Integrity

Before return, the box should be:

- validated;
- signed;
- content-addressed where applicable;
- versioned;
- tamper-evident;
- linked to its source request;
- given a terminal or next-gate state.

No unsealed or unowned result may enter collaboration or commerce.

- open metadata;
- discovery;
- registries;
- shared context;
- common-cause clusters;
- invitations;
- review;
- handoffs;
- versioning;
- attribution;

Collaboration does not require the platform to absorb ownership of local work or private intelligence.

Platform Commerce

## Single Ownership

Every box has exactly one active owner at every point in time.

During collaboration or commerce:

- custody may change;
- operators may change;
- reviewers may change;
- recipients may receive licensed rights;
- contributors may receive value;

but ownership changes only through an explicit atomic transfer contract.

## Privacy and Local Sovereignty

The platform must not require unrestricted visibility into the user’s home.

It should use:

- minimum necessary evidence;
- selective disclosure;
- local validation;
- signed attestations;
- privacy-preserving proofs;
- bounded telemetry;
- explicit consent;
- retention limits;
- revocation;
- local custody.

```text
No Operations in the Dark
    applies to the contracted work

No Surveillance of the Home
    applies to unrelated private life and methods
```

## Native Agents

Agents operating inside the home must be native to that home environment.

They require:

- local identity;
- local tool access;
- local contracts;
- local ownership and accountability;
- local heartbeat;
- local recovery;
- explicit scope.

A platform agent may deliver or inspect the box through the gate, but it does not gain general authority inside the home.

## Partner Enablement

Partners may support:

- tools;
- materials;
- infrastructure;
- logistics;
- validation;
- packaging;
- labeling;
- distribution;
- support;
- settlement.

Each partner receives only the minimum role and context required by its contract.

## Failure and Recovery

If the box cannot be opened, completed, validated, or repacked:

```text
Pause
→ Preserve Box and Local State
→ Notify Named Parties
→ Record Failure Evidence
→ Revoke Temporary Access
→ Retry, Return, Reassign, or Close
```

The user must never be trapped in an unfinished commercial or operational relationship without a defined exit.

## Platform Responsibilities

The platform must:

- register requests, boxes, users, homes, and partners;
- preserve box identity and integrity;
- enforce sealed transit;
- validate home-gate opening;
- avoid unnecessary intrusion into local work;
- preserve single ownership;
- support labeling and provenance;
- enable collaboration and commerce;
- meter and settle value transparently;
- preserve contributor attribution;
- support return, recovery, dispute, and exit.

## Platform Invariants

1. The box remains sealed during transit.
2. The box opens only in the intended recipient's trusted home environment.
3. The home retains local sovereignty, ownership, privacy, and tool choice.
4. Platform authority at the home gate does not become universal home authority.
5. The user may use real-world native tools within the accepted contract.
6. The result is repacked, labeled, signed, and evidenced before return.
7. Private methods and unrelated local data remain protected.
8. Every box has one active owner at all times.
9. The platform enables collaboration and commerce without capturing ownership.
10. Every transfer, delivery, acceptance, settlement, and exit is contract-bound and attributable.

## Final Statement

> Let the box remain sealed until it reaches the end user’s trusted home. Let the user open it there, work with their own real-world tools, knowledge, judgment, and creativity, then pack the result back into the box with clear labels, provenance, rights, quality evidence, and ownership. The platform should provide the trusted space for discovery, collaboration, delivery, and commerce—empowering the user without entering, owning, or entangling their home.
