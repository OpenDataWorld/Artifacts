# Local Distributed Social Network for Digital Nomads

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> A Local Distributed Social Network for Digital Nomads is a local-first, federated social system in which people retain portable identity, local community membership, lawful jurisdiction, trusted neighbourhoods, and the right to move without surrendering ownership of their relationships, work, data, or reputation.

The network is distributed across local communities, cities, regions, nations, organizations, and digital platforms. Federation happens at trusted gates. Identity travels through verifiable credentials and explicit consent, not through permanent dependence on one central platform.

## Core Rule

> Be local where you are. Remain portable when you move. Federate through trusted gates. Preserve identity, context, rights, and provenance.

## Local First

Each local network should support:

- local identity verification;
- local communities and trusted circles;
- local services;
- local opportunities;
- local events;
- local governance;
- local data custody where appropriate;
- offline or degraded operation;
- local moderation;
- local dispute resolution;
- local economic exchange.

Local-first does not mean isolated. It means the local community remains the primary context and authority for local participation.

## Distributed Network

The network may consist of independent nodes operated by:

- municipalities;
- co-working communities;
- universities;
- professional associations;
- local businesses;
- cooperatives;
- creator communities;
- travel communities;
- public institutions;
- private platforms;
- digital nomad groups;
- national or regional networks.

Each node remains independently governed and interoperates through the Fabric Protocol and Trusted Gate Protocol.

## Digital Nomad Identity

A digital nomad should carry a portable Personal Kube containing:

- verified identity;
- home jurisdiction;
- current location or locality context where voluntarily disclosed;
- professional skills;
- agent and platform profiles;
- credentials;
- work history;
- contribution history;
- trust and reputation evidence;
- memberships;
- rights and permissions;
- consent preferences;
- emergency and recovery contacts;
- lifecycle and revocation state.

```text
Personal Kube
=
Identity
+ Context
+ Skills
+ Credentials
+ Contributions
+ Rights
+ Provenance
```

## Local Membership

Joining a local network requires:

1. identity resolution;
2. current local context;
3. accepted community contract;
4. consent;
5. eligibility and ability where relevant;
6. rights and responsibilities;
7. moderation and safety rules;
8. data-sharing preferences;
9. expiry or review period;
10. exit path.

Membership remains contextual and does not create universal access across the federation.

## Trusted Neighbourhoods

Local networks define digital neighbours through explicit federation contracts.

```text
Local Network A
    ↓ Trusted Gate
Federation Contract
    ↓ Trusted Gate
Local Network B
```

Neighbour relationships may support:

- trusted introductions;
- reciprocal community access;
- work opportunities;
- temporary residency support;
- service discovery;
- emergency assistance;
- learning and cultural exchange;
- common-cause collaboration;
- professional verification;
- contribution portability.

## Common Cause Clusters

Digital nomads may form Common Cause Clusters around:

- projects;
- research;
- local improvement;
- volunteering;
- entrepreneurship;
- creator work;
- public-interest initiatives;
- emergency response;
- cultural exchange;
- shared learning;
- temporary teams.

Every cluster must have a registered cause, eligible and able members, explicit roles, collective outcome responsibility, metered usage where applicable, and a defined dissolution path.

## Portable Reputation

Reputation must be based on evidence rather than platform popularity alone.

```text
Portable Reputation
=
Verified Contributions
+ Completed Kubes
+ Trusted Handoffs
+ Contextual References
+ Preserved Provenance
```

A local network may evaluate reputation independently. Trust earned in one context does not automatically become universal trust in another.

## Work and Opportunity

The network may support:

- local jobs;
- freelance work;
- project teams;
- verified service providers;
- skill exchange;
- mentorship;
- temporary assignments;
- remote work communities;
- contributor programs;
- local entrepreneurship.

Every commercial or consequential interaction must pass rights, jurisdiction, eligibility, ability, contract, metering, and settlement checks.

## Economic Model

The network should use a transparent and sustainable model based on:

- metered commercial usage;
- fair and safe-use policies;
- contributor support;
- local service fees;
- community memberships;
- marketplace commissions where declared;
- sponsorship;
- public-interest subsidies;
- managed enterprise services;
- certification or verification fees.

Open community participation must not silently become unpaid commercial labor.

## Contributor Support

At every publishing, work, or distribution gate, the platform should preserve:

- contributor identity;
- contribution type;
- attribution;
- license;
- commercial rights;
- compensation terms;
- settlement evidence;
- dispute path.

## Privacy

The network must minimize location and identity exposure.

It should support:

- selective disclosure;
- local verification;
- pseudonymous participation for low-consequence contexts;
- explicit consent;
- time-bound location sharing;
- minimum necessary data;
- local custody;
- revocation;
- deletion or retention controls;
- emergency disclosure only under valid authority.

A social network for digital nomads must not become a movement-tracking system.

## Safety

Every local node should implement:

- community safety rules;
- identity and impersonation controls;
- abuse reporting;
- block and deny rights;
- conflict resolution;
- moderation transparency;
- appeal;
- emergency support;
- fraud controls;
- safe meeting guidance;
- platform eligibility for agents and automated services.

## Agentic Participation

Agents may assist with:

- local discovery;
- translation;
- scheduling;
- opportunity matching;
- travel coordination;
- work handoffs;
- service navigation;
- local knowledge;
- community moderation support;
- contribution tracking.

Agents must remain domain-bound, jurisdiction-bound, maturity-bound, and platform-eligible. They may not infer universal authority from a user's membership or location.

## Right to Reason and Right to Deny

Every member retains:

- the Right to Reason about recommendations, restrictions, prices, moderation, and gate decisions;
- the Right to Deny access, handoffs, invitations, data sharing, and federation requests where lawful;
- the right to challenge;
- the right to appeal;
- the right to exit;
- the right to port identity and evidence.

## Federation and Nations

A nation may participate as a sovereign member of the federation through its own lawful gates.

Local networks must respect:

- immigration and residency law;
- employment law;
- taxation;
- consumer protection;
- data protection;
- professional licensing;
- local safety rules;
- constitutional and human rights.

Digital mobility does not erase real-world authority or jurisdiction.

## Local Node Lifecycle

```text
proposed
→ published
→ registered
→ admitted
→ active
→ reviewed
→ renewed
→ restricted
→ suspended
→ retired
→ archived
```

No stale or unmaintained community node should remain trusted indefinitely.

## Network Invariants

1. The network is local-first and federated, not centrally sovereign.
2. Every member carries a portable, consent-bound identity.
3. Every local node remains independently governed.
4. Federation occurs only through Trusted Gates.
5. Local membership never creates universal authority.
6. Reputation is based on evidence and remains contextual.
7. Privacy, selective disclosure, and lawful exit are mandatory.
8. Agents remain bounded by domain, maturity, jurisdiction, and platform eligibility.
9. Commercial usage is transparent, metered, rights-cleared, and contributor-supporting.
10. Communities may form Common Cause Clusters without losing local sovereignty.
11. Every consequential interaction preserves instruction, evidence, and provenance.
12. The network supports movement without turning mobility into surveillance.

## Final Statement

> A Local Distributed Social Network for Digital Nomads allows people to belong locally while remaining portable globally. Identity, work, relationships, and reputation move through trusted gates rather than being trapped inside one platform. Local communities retain governance, nations retain jurisdiction, contributors retain rights, and digital nomads retain the freedom to move, participate, reason, deny, and exit.
