# Nation as a Member of the Federation

Status: Normative Foundation Principle

## Principle

> The nation is part of the federation.

A nation participates as a sovereign, lawful, accountable member of the wider federated Fabric. It does not stand outside the federation as an unrestricted controller, and it does not surrender its constitutional identity, jurisdiction, public institutions, rights, or lawful authority by joining.

## Core Rule

> Federate through lawful national gates. Preserve sovereignty. Share only what the federation contract requires. Retain the right to reason, deny, review, recover, and exit.

## Canonical Model

```text
Nation A
  ↓ National Trusted Gate
Federation Contract
  ↓ Federated Trusted Gate
Nation B / Organization / Platform / Domain
```

The federation is formed from independent members whose boundaries remain visible and governed.

## The Nation Kube

Within the Fabric, a nation resolves to a high-order Kube containing:

- constitutional identity;
- people and public purpose;
- territory and jurisdiction;
- lawful institutions;
- statutory agencies;
- public infrastructure;
- national contracts and obligations;
- rights and protections;
- public data stewardship;
- security and resilience responsibilities;
- provenance;
- lifecycle and lawful continuity.

```text
Nation Kube
=
People
+ Constitutional Identity
+ Jurisdiction
+ Institutions
+ Rights
+ Public Responsibilities
+ Provenance
```

## Federation at the Gate

Federation occurs only through national and federated gates.

Every crossing must resolve:

```text
Identity
Context
Contract
Jurisdiction
Eligibility
Ability
Responsibility
Provenance
Exit
```

No external member receives direct access to the internal national Fabric merely because a federation relationship exists.

## What the Nation Retains

The nation retains:

- constitutional sovereignty;
- jurisdiction;
- public accountability;
- national security responsibilities;
- data and infrastructure custody;
- admission and denial rights;
- statutory authority;
- cultural and community protections;
- independent review and appeal structures;
- lawful exit from a federation contract.

## What the Nation Shares

A nation may share only the minimum necessary:

- interoperable identity assertions;
- public standards;
- agreed safety requirements;
- bounded delegations;
- conformance evidence;
- treaty or federation obligations;
- incident coordination data;
- mutually accepted service interfaces;
- provenance needed for trust.

Sharing must remain purpose-bound, lawful, proportionate, and revocable.

## Nation First, Federation Second

```text
People and Nation
        ↓
Constitutional Governance
        ↓
National Gate
        ↓
Federation Participation
```

Federation cannot override the lawful constitutional order of a member nation unless that nation has explicitly accepted the obligation through a valid legal process.

## Statutory Agencies in the Federation

Statutory agencies participate through their lawful mandates.

```text
Delegated Agent Authority
≤
Agency Authority
≤
Statutory Mandate
≤
Constitutional Boundary
```

An agency cannot use federation membership to expand its mandate beyond national law.

## Agentic Enablement

Agents acting for a nation or statutory agency require:

- verified public or institutional identity;
- explicit mandate or delegation;
- jurisdiction mapping;
- platform eligibility;
- demonstrated ability;
- signed attestation;
- human accountability;
- instruction and provenance evidence;
- expiry, revocation, review, and remedy.

Federation does not grant universal authority to national agents.

## Separation of Duties

National and federated decisions should separate:

- national policy definition;
- treaty or federation approval;
- technical implementation;
- operational execution;
- conformance validation;
- audit;
- dispute resolution;
- recovery and exit.

Duties remain separated while the nation and federation collectively own their respective obligations.

## Rights of a Federated Nation

Every nation retains:

- Right to Reason;
- Right to Deny where lawful;
- right to challenge a federation decision;
- right to require evidence;
- right to restrict or revoke access;
- right to protect proprietary or classified intelligence;
- right to preserve local law and rights;
- right to recover and exit under the federation contract.

## Obligations of a Federated Nation

A participating nation must:

- honor accepted federation contracts;
- preserve evidence and provenance;
- disclose relevant limitations;
- maintain accountable national gates;
- prevent unauthorized cross-border authority;
- support review and remedy;
- protect other members from unsafe or nonconformant operation;
- retire stale, expired, or revoked relationships.

## Federation Trust

Trust is not automatic between nations.


## Exit and Suspension

Every federation relationship must define:

- suspension triggers;
- emergency restriction;
- dispute process;
- data and state return;
- continuity obligations;
- settlement;
- migration;
- archival provenance;
- lawful exit.

No nation should become trapped in a federation without a defined exit path.

## Platform Invariants

1. A nation participates as a sovereign member of the federation.
2. The nation is not an unrestricted controller above the federation.
3. Federation occurs at lawful national and shared gates.
4. National constitutional order and jurisdiction remain visible.
5. Only minimum necessary information and authority cross the boundary.
6. Federation does not create universal agent authority.
7. Statutory agencies remain bounded by national law.
8. Every crossing preserves responsibility, evidence, and provenance.
9. Every member retains rights to reason, deny, review, recover, and exit.
10. Trust is continuously earned through reciprocal conformance and accountable delivery.

## Final Statement

> The nation is part of the federation as a sovereign, accountable member. It enters through its own lawful gate, accepts only explicit federation contracts, preserves constitutional governance and jurisdiction, shares the minimum necessary, and retains the right to reason, deny, review, recover, and exit. Federation connects nations without erasing them.
